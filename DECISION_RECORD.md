# Decision Record

## 2026-07-18 — Build a developer tool, not another game demo

**Decision:** Submit PlayReceipt to the Developer Tools track.

**Why:** A game by itself would compete mostly on content polish and would hide the strongest new technical contribution. The reusable problem is the gap between a passing build and a defensible play-quality verdict.

**Alternatives rejected:**

- Submit the existing game: too much pre-event work and the current evidence is honestly `REPAIR`.
- Build a general AI-agent observability dashboard: broader, but less distinctive and harder to demonstrate rigorously in three minutes.
- Collapse all quality into one score: visually convenient but dishonest about human fun calibration.

**Source preservation:** The original `NEO-GENESIS: OVERCLOCK` repository is read-only for this submission. Three evidence artifacts are copied under `examples/source/overclock/`; their content is not rewritten.

**Disclosure:** PlayReceipt is AI-assisted work. No public AI-origin label is required for the product UI; internal provenance and submission disclosures will accurately describe the use of Codex and GPT-5.6.
